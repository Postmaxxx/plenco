Plenco Assists Black & Decker™ with New Product Line
November 01, 2021 at 2:55 PM CST

Technical Service, Tooling and Material Engineering Supports Quickpress Iron

SHEBOYGAN, Wisconsin – (November 01, 2021) – Black and Decker™ was in the process of developing and launching a new steam iron into the electric iron market.

The Engineering Manager of Black & Decker contacted Plastics Engineering Company (Plenco) to assist them with various production issues that they encountered using a competitor’s material. It was decided to bring the new steam iron tool into Plenco’s technical molding facility where it was evaluated and found to have incorporated many thermoplastic design characteristics. With four additional tools in que to be built due to the large anticipated volume for the new model, time was of critical importance.

Plenco’s Technical Service Engineers reviewed the tool and CAD models, and a plan was quickly communicated to Black and Decker’s engineering team. The current tool being built from stainless steel presented issues where the welding, machining and blending of twelve vents was required to yield well-molded parts. Knowing the price sensitivity of this project, Plenco’s Tool Room built and installed a new center block with a reduced size runner system, installed a 7/32” sprue bushing and machined three vents at the end of the cavity. Thermocouple holes were drilled into four strategic locations and optimum sized heating rods were installed.

While the tool work was being performed, Plenco’s Materials Engineering Lab formulated Plenco 07466 black, a flock reinforced, two-stage phenolic molding compound.

Plenco 07466 black proved to be capable of meeting the impact and moisture resistance Black & Decker required.

Plenco’s extensive processing knowledge was put to the test to engineer a robust process with a four-cavity tool requiring a 42-second clamp-to-clamp cycle. The process required:

  • Breathing the mold during primary injection to eliminate a gas burn on the water supply nipple
  • Maintaining minimal die opening to avoid thick parting line
  • Yielding a part with no breathe marks on the visible portion of the iron skirt

The results of support, experience and effort that Plenco’s technical team put forth was a complete success. Black and Decker built the four additional tools incorporating all of the design suggestions and experienced great success in the molding and sales of millions of steam irons.
